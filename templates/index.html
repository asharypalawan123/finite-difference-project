Numerical Differentiation using Forward, Backward, and Central Difference Methods

Mathematical Discussion

Finite difference methods are numerical techniques used to approximate derivatives using nearby function values.

Forward Difference

\[ f'(x) \approx \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \]

Backward Difference

\[ f'(x) \approx \frac{f(x)-f(x-h)}{h} \]

Central Difference

\[ f'(x) \approx \frac{f(x+h)-f(x-h)}{2h} \]

Worked Example 1

Given:

\[ f(x)=x^2 \]

\[ x=2 \]

\[ h=0.1 \]

\[ f'(x) \approx \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \]

\[ f'(2) \approx \frac{4.41-4}{0.1} \]

\[ f'(2) \approx 4.1 \]

Worked Example 2

Given:

\[ f(x)=\sin(x) \]

\[ x=1 \]

\[ h=0.01 \]

\[ f'(x) \approx \frac{f(x+h)-f(x-h)}{2h} \]

\[ f'(1) \approx 0.54 \]
